About

What I Do

I'm a PhD student at the University of Toronto's Daniels Faculty of Architecture, Landscape and Design. I study spatial analytics for environmental science. I also work in the Faculty of Law in the IT Department. Feel free to contact me if you have any questions about my work! I'm supervised by Dr. Patrick James.

Spatial Data Analytics

I have extensive experience in spatial analysis, including preparing large geographic datasets, completing tasks in mapping software, and applying advanced statistical and machine learning methods to analyze trends. I've taken geocomputing, quantitative methods, advanced statistics, and spatial statistics courses at the graduate level.

Python, R, C++, ArcMap, QGIS

Environmental Science

Most of my work focuses on applying spatial analysis and cartography to environmental science problems, including designing a method using satellite imagery and machine learning to map tree damage across Ontario and completing a research project on mapping fire season start and end dates.


Forest Health, Forest Fire Risk, Insect Damage Risk

Map Design & Data Prep

I have an MS degree from UW-Madison in GIS & Cartography. I've since applied the design principles I learned there to communicate research findings. As a Research Assistant, I create maps for presentations and publications and prepare large geographic datasets for professors, postdocs, and graduate students.

JavaScript / HTML / CSS, Adobe Illustrator, MayaVi
